Formula Student

Formula Student is Europe’s most prominent educational motorsport event. It challenges engineers to design, build and compete as a team with their single racing car. The annual event run by the Institution of Mechanical Engineers (IMechE) attracts 95 teams representing universities from all corners of the globe. It challenges students to design, build and race a one-person, open-wheeled race car assuming that a manufacturing firm has engaged them to produce a prototype car for evaluation as a potential production item. The team’s hard work culminates in July at the actual Formula Student event, which takes place at the famous Silverst one Racing Circuit. It is here that teams demonstrate to the judges, in a series of events, that the car is fit for purpose and presenting a business plan that sells their concept to potential investors. The Formula Student competition features two main disciplines, the Static Events and the Dynamic Events.

Participants from five continents

  • North America

Static Events

Engineering Design

An engineering design report is handed in to the jury before the event starts and defended at a design presentation in front of expert judges. Evaluated are not only the use of new materials and creative assembly technologies, but also the overall construction and weight of the vehicle.

Cost report

Costs are an important factor for building a race car. Hence, the students deal with cost estimations, manufacturing techniques and processes in the Cost Event. The discipline consists of a written report (the Cost Report) and a discussion with the judges around the manufactured prototype. The Cost Report contains a list of all components.

Business plan presentation

With a clever business plan and a convincing sales talk, the students are supposed to sell a short run order of their race car to a fictional investment group, represented by the jury.
